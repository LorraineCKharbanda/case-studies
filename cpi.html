Case Study 01

Customer Product Interlock (CPI)

Cross-Functional AI Workflow Redesign for Program Visibility & Reporting
The Problem

Multiple cross-functional teams (Sales/GTM, Customer Success, Engineering, Product) were operating with fragmented, manual workflows across Salesforce, ServiceNow, Jira, and Monday.com. No one had visibility into who was working on what. Escalations were reactive. Planning was impossible because there was no structured reporting, no shared prioritization framework, and no automated tracking of work status across teams.

Average processing time per request was 60 to 240 minutes, with multi-week to multi-month delays. Leadership meetings were driven by gut feeling rather than data. Feature requests, bugs, and escalations lived in separate systems with no reconciliation.

My Role

As Senior Technical Program Manager, I led the design and implementation of a unified, AI-powered workflow layer. I mapped end-to-end workflows across all teams, audited every system, identified bottlenecks and high-value automation opportunities, and created business requirements for a fully interconnected workflow engine.

What I Built

I designed and deployed an LLM-driven workflow engine that automated intake, classification, routing, scoring, ticket generation, and communication across all four platforms.

Systems Integration (Salesforce, ServiceNow, Jira, Monday.com)

Unified four disconnected platforms into a single automated pipeline. The integration layer was the foundation, every downstream capability depended on cross-system data reconciliation, deduplication, and synchronized state. Before this, teams were manually copying data between systems. After, a single input triggered automated workflows across all four platforms simultaneously.

Logarithmic Stack-Ranking Algorithm

A four-part scoring system I designed that aligned every feature request to business goals, revenue impact, product strategy, and operational urgency, replacing subjective prioritization with transparent, measurable ranking.

Automated Intake & Classification

The system parsed customer input, classified request type (bug, feature, risk, escalation), identified duplicates across systems, and generated complete Jira tickets with full metadata.

Real-Time Reporting & Visibility

Automated cross-system data reconciliation produced structured insights for leadership, enabling 30-minute monthly escalation reviews with transparent agendas instead of multi-hour fire drills.

Automated Communication

Customer-facing and internal updates triggered immediately on request submission, reducing client communication turnaround from 4 months to 3 weeks.

Relevance
Maps directly to any organization needing program-level visibility across distributed engineering and operations teams.
Demonstrates building reporting infrastructure where none existed, replacing ad hoc coordination with structured, automated roll-ups across complex multi-team programs.
Shows how to move cross-functional programs from reactive firefighting to proactive, data-driven planning with measurable impact on program-level OKRs.
