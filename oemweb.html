Case Study 05

Data Governance Under Federal Judicial Oversight

OEMWeb, OCE & ECE Platform Management for the Microsoft Antitrust Consent Decree
The Problem

Following Congress's antitrust decision against Microsoft, the company operated under a federal consent decree that required full transparency and auditability of its OEM partner ecosystem. The platform supported over 17,000 OEM partners across hardware, mobile, and embedded devices. Every interaction between Microsoft and these partners, including pricing documents, software updates, data downloads, newsletters, and official communications, had to be tracked, versioned, and available for judicial review at any time. There was zero tolerance for data governance failures.

My Role

As Technical Program & Web Operations Manager at Accenture/Cayuse Holdings, I led technical programs, platform operations, and release management for the OEMWeb, OCE (OEM Communications Engine), and ECE (Enterprise Communications Engine) platforms. These were the primary channels through which Microsoft managed its OEM partner relationships under the consent decree.

What I Built
Compliance Platform Architecture

Translated complex regulatory requirements into product specifications, including attestation workflows, audit trails, and periodic revalidation mechanisms that ensured every partner interaction was documented and traceable.

Data Provenance & Audit Infrastructure

Built systems to track the full lifecycle of every pricing document, software download, newsletter, and communication sent to OEM partners. Every data action was logged with who accessed it, when, what version, and whether it met compliance requirements.

Cross-Functional Coordination

Coordinated across legal, procurement, engineering, and business stakeholders to deliver compliant software systems on schedule, operating at the intersection of technical delivery and regulatory governance.

Release Management Under Constraint

Managed release cadences for platforms under judicial oversight, where any release failure or data integrity issue could have regulatory consequences. Transitioned the web development team from Waterfall to Agifall to pure Agile methodology while maintaining compliance standards.

Results

Maintained full compliance with the federal consent decree throughout the engagement. Every OEM partner interaction was auditable, versioned, and available for judicial review on demand. Zero compliance failures on data governance. Successfully transitioned development methodology while maintaining regulatory requirements. The platforms served as the backbone of Microsoft's OEM partner ecosystem during one of the most scrutinized periods in the company's history.

The Governance Principle
When data governance is not optional, when there are real consequences for losing provenance, for serving the wrong version, for not knowing who accessed what, you build differently. You build systems where tracking, metadata, and lineage are structural, not afterthoughts. That discipline is transferable to any environment where shared data assets need integrity, including engineering teams sharing infrastructure and data across an organization at scale.
Relevance
Demonstrates building and maintaining data governance infrastructure where every data action, download, pricing document, and communication had to be tracked, cataloged, and audit-ready.
Shows experience with provenance-level operational management: who accessed what, when, what version, and whether it was compliant.
Directly relevant to any organization managing shared resources across multiple teams with rigorous tracking, lifecycle management, and operational governance.
