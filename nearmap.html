Case Study 02

Transforming Satellite Imagery Annotation for NearMap

Scaling a High-Complexity Data Collection Pipeline from Crisis to Growth
The Problem

NearMap's AI annotation pipeline was in crisis. The operation, which converted raw satellite imagery into structured roofline geometries for real-time roofing material estimation, was underperforming across every dimension: throughput delays, 78% workforce attrition, and $700K per month in late penalties. NearMap had explicitly told us they would not expand volume until annotation speed doubled without quality loss.

The root causes were systemic: no real-time visibility into data throughput or quality metrics, a linear training model that took 3 months to onboard annotators, outdated hardware creating zoom latency that degraded both speed and precision, and no infrastructure for surge capacity.

My Role

As Senior Program Manager for AI Data Services at Appen, I managed an $18M portfolio including this engagement. My role was to optimize the workflow into a scalable, productized AI annotation pipeline that could meet enterprise SLAs, double throughput, and eliminate penalties while maintaining gold-standard quality.

What I Built
Data Transparency & Tracking

Partnered with analytics teams to launch real-time reporting dashboards integrating both Appen and NearMap datasets, creating full transparency into speed, accuracy, and volume. This transformed weekly client syncs from reactive status updates into insight-driven business reviews.

Workflow Redesign

Partnered with NearMap Product and Engineering to rebuild the annotator training system from a linear, gold-set model into a modular, property-type-based framework, reducing onboarding from 3 months to 3 weeks.

Infrastructure Optimization

Upgraded to integrated GPU-accelerated Apple workstations with 5K monitors, allowing us to surpass RAM limitations, reduce zoom latency, and boost annotation precision. Conducted A/B testing to validate cycle-time and accuracy improvements.

Scalable Human-in-the-Loop System

Designed a rolling-shift global model for 24/7 coverage with buffer teams to absorb ad hoc surges (disaster-response imagery) without quality degradation.

Engineering Feedback Loop

Established structured touchpoints connecting operations, product, and dev teams for UAT testing, iteration, and platform enhancements, future-proofing the annotation platform.
